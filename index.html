    From the wistfulness of the unyielding sand to the fragrance of the rain swept fertile soil - Mrittika has been shaped in an exhilarating endeavour to capture and portray all this and more, through music.
    Mrittika, with its base in Hindustani and Carnatic classical, seeks to transcend all borders and celebrate the free spirit of music, by drawing influences from styles as varied as the myraid sounds of Indian folk to the groovy notes of American jazz. These influences come together in music that incorporates foot tapping rhythms and soul stirring melodies.
    One of the principle aims of the band is to bring to the forefront the various unnoticed musical styles from every nook and corner of the subcontinent.
    Being a fairly new ensemble, Mrittika has already featured in the MTV Coke Studio auditions at the Blue Frog (Delhi). This was soon followed by an invitation to the prestigious TV show Music Mojo on Kappa TV, on a platform already graced by stalwarts like Rashid Ali, Neha Nair, Baiju Dharmajan. The episode featuring Mrittika was aired in August 2013. The band has since been performing at various venues all across the country.
    The first landmark opportunity to have its name associated with a silverscreen venture came by Mrittika's way when the band composed the title track for a Malyalam movie Masala Republic which released in May, 2014.

     
    Madhubanti Bagchi
    Trained in Hindustan Classical music and also its various sub-genres like Thumris, Ghazals for more than 16 years, the soulful voice of this Mrittika vocalist has also been featured as playback in the works of national award winning music directors.
    Suchal Chakraborty
    Having been a student for more than a decade now, Suchal, a classical music exponent, not only renders his voice to Mrittika but has also performed as a solo artist all over the country. He has released an album from Black and White Records in association with InReco.
    Nilanjan Ghosh
    Being trained in both Indian and Western instruments, Nilanjan carries out the percussive duties in the band. As a solo artist, he has played with the maestros of Indian Classical music as well as reputed music directors of the industry.
    Diptangshu Chakrabarty
    Mrittika's guitarist - He comes with an in-depth knowledge of the Hawaiian Lap Steel Guitar and the Spanish Guitar. The band's music imbibes the Jazz influences from him.
    Shouvik Mukherjee
    Plays the Zitar, the modern take on the elegant Sitar, for the band. Having performed in India and abroad, he has been a significant addition to the band's rich oriental sound.
    Sudipto Sarkar
    Supports the low end with influences ranging from Jazz to Heavy Metal. He has shared the stage with numerous well known musicians of Kolkata.
    Zafar Ansari
    Nurturing a long standing tryst with the piano that dates back to very a tender age, Zafar has explored genres like Funk, Blues and Jazz during his time at The Swarnabhoomi Academy of Music.
